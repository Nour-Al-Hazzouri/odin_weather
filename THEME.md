## Color Palettes (HEX Codes)## ☀️ Sunny & Clear

- Background Gradient: #4A90E2 (Sky Blue) to #50E3C2 (Mint)
- Text & Icons: #0A2540 (Deep Navy) [1]

## 🌧️ Rainy & Stormy

- Background Gradient: #4F5D75 (Slate Gray) to #2D3142 (Dark Charcoal)
- Text & Icons: #EF8354 (Warm Coral) or #FFFFFF (Crisp White)

## ❄️ Snowy & Ice

- Background Gradient: #E0EAFC (Icy Ice) to #CFDEF3 (Pale Blue)
- Text & Icons: #2B5876 (Deep Pacific Blue)

## ☁️ Cloudy & Overcast

- Background Gradient: #757F9A (Muted Gray) to #D7DDE8 (Light Silver)
- Text & Icons: #1F2421 (Off-Black)

## 🌙 Clear Night [2]

- Background Gradient: #0B132B (Midnight) to #1C2541 (Deep Space)
- Text & Icons: #FFD166 (Warm Glow Yellow)

## Where to Find Weather Icons## 1. Lucide Icons (Highly Recommended) [3]

- Why: Modern, clean, and consistent vector outlines.
- Selection: Excellent dedicated weather set (Sun, Moon, CloudRain, Wind, Drizzle).
- Customization: Easy to recolor via software or directly in design tools. [4, 5]

## 2. Remix Icon

- Why: Huge open-source library with both outline and filled styles.
- Selection: Offers a dedicated "Weather" category with unique night/day variations.

## 3. Font Awesome

- Why: The industry standard for web projects.
- Selection: Thousands of free icons, including standard temperature, wind, and droplet shapes. [6]

---

## Visual & Layout Tips

- Group by Contrast: Keep text and icons the exact same color per theme to make the interface look unified.
- Spacing Matters: Add a small gap between the icon and the text inside your paragraphs so they do not overlap.
- Match Weights: Choose a line-based (outline) icon style if your font is thin, or a filled icon style if your font is bold.
- Use Visual Hierarchy: Make the city name and temperature font size larger than the humidity and wind speed text. [7, 8, 9]
